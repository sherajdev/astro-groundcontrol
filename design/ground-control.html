Ground
Control.

A calm, editorial dev portfolio with one comic panel, a muted steel palette, and a ghosted Tron terminal for the code. Restraint everywhere — drama in exactly one place.

retro-cartoon restraint muted steel / grey / ink ghosted terminal
01 the idea

Three notes, played quietly.

Each reference contributes one thing. The comic gives character, the editorial grid gives calm, the deck gives the muted-blue mood. Held together by restraint — the system says "no" far more than it says "yes."

/ 01

Retro-cartoon, restrained

Ink-line character shows up once — your avatar in a comic panel. Everywhere else the line work cools into hairlines and halftone dots. The cartoon is a guest, not the host.

/ 02

Muted steel palette

Dusty blue, warm grey, soft black, off-white paper. No saturation contests. One burnt-orange spot — borrowed from the rocket — gets a strict budget of one appearance per page.

/ 03

Ghosted Tron terminal

Code lives in a near-black panel with a faint blue grid, low-glow cyan text, and drifting scanlines. Ghosted, not neon — the glow whispers.

02 color

Paper light. Steel cool. Ink soft.

The light UI carries everything. Avoid pure black (#000) and pure white — soft-black ink on warm-grey paper reads calmer and ages better.

The one-spot rule. Burnt orange appears a single time on any given page — an active link, a status dot, a hover. That scarcity is what makes the muted palette feel intentional instead of timid.

04 the one comic moment

Your avatar earns the only cartoon on the site.

Framed as a comic panel — ink border, hard offset shadow, halftone backdrop, a slight tilt. It's the human warmth in an otherwise systematic layout. Use it in the hero or about section; never repeat the ink-line treatment elsewhere.

treatment recipe

• 3px soft-black border (#15171B)
• hard offset shadow, no blur (7px 7px 0)
• steel-soft halftone panel behind, counter-rotated
• ~1.6° tilt for the hand-placed comic feel
• mono caption tab breaking the frame
• the orange in the art = your one spot color

07 principles

The rules that keep it calm.

P1

One loud thing per screen

The avatar, or the terminal, or a spot of orange. Never two at once. Quiet makes the one loud thing land.

P2

Mono is the connective tissue

Every label, date, tag, and nav item is mono. It threads the "machine" feeling through the calm editorial body.

P3

Hairlines over boxes

Separate with 1px lines and space, not heavy borders or shadows. Let the grid do the structural work.

P4

Texture, not decoration

Halftone and grid add atmosphere at low opacity. If a texture announces itself, dial it down.
